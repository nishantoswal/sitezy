Do I Need a Website If I Already Have a Facebook Page?

Having a Facebook page is a start. But relying on it as your main online presence is costing you customers — every single day.

Almost every small business has some form of social media presence. And almost every owner has, at some point, wondered: do I really need a website too? The short answer is yes — and here is exactly why.

Facebook Does Not Show Up in Google the Way a Website Does

When someone Googles "plumber in Springfield" or "Italian restaurant near me," they are looking for a business to hire or visit right now. A Facebook page rarely ranks well for those searches. A purpose-built, SEO-optimised website does. If you do not have one, you are invisible to the people who are actively trying to find you — and those are the highest-converting customers on the internet.

Facebook Owns Your Audience. You Do Not.

Every follower you have on Facebook belongs to Meta, not to you. If Facebook changes its algorithm, reduces your organic reach, or suspends your account, your audience disappears overnight. A website is an asset you own. Your email list, your content, your leads — they all live on infrastructure you control. No platform can take that away.

"A Facebook page gives you a presence. A website gives you an asset."

Serious Buyers Check for a Website Before They Call

Think about your own buying behaviour. When you get a recommendation or find a business online, what do you do? You look for their website. If there is no website — or the site looks outdated and unprofessional — your confidence drops immediately. The same happens with your potential customers. A missing or poor website loses the sale before the conversation even starts.

You Cannot Capture Leads Properly on Facebook

A website can have a contact form that sends enquiries to your inbox 24/7, a booking system, a live chat widget, a lead magnet, or an email capture popup. Facebook gives you a "Message" button. The lead capture capability of a proper website is simply not comparable. You are leaving money on the table every day you do not have one.

What to Do About It

Keep your Facebook page — social media has genuine value for community building, retargeting ads, and word-of-mouth amplification. But treat it as a supplement to your website, not a substitute for it. Your website is home base. Social media channels are signposts that point people back to it.
