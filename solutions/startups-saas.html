The Real Problem

Why good products
don't sell themselves online

Poor product architecture loses buyers

If customers can't find what they're looking for in two clicks, they leave. A disorganised category structure and unclear navigation silently kills conversion rates every day.

Checkout breaks on mobile

Over 60% of purchases start on mobile. A checkout flow that's clunky on a phone means lost sales at the final step — right when the customer has already decided to buy.

No organic search traffic

Without proper SEO on your category and product pages, you're invisible to buyers who are actively searching for what you sell. All your revenue depends on paid ads you can't turn off.

Generic design loses niche credibility

Niche buyers are discerning. A template that looks like everyone else signals you're not serious about your product. Customers in specialist markets buy from brands that look like they belong.

Common Questions

Questions from
online store owners

Shopify or WooCommerce — which do you recommend?
It depends on your catalogue size and technical preferences. For most brands under a few hundred SKUs, we recommend Shopify for its reliability and mobile checkout. For larger, more complex stores that need deeper customisation, WooCommerce gives more flexibility. We'll recommend the right fit for your specific situation.
Can you migrate my existing store to a new site?
Yes. If you have an existing store, we can migrate your products, collections, and content to the new site. We'll scope the migration as part of the initial brief — no data gets lost, and we handle the technical side entirely.
How do you handle product updates and new arrivals?
All product additions, price changes, image updates, and description changes are included in your monthly plan. Send us the details and we'll have them live within 24 hours. For high-volume catalogue updates, we can discuss a bulk update process.
Can you build SEO into the product and category pages?
Yes — SEO is built into every page we create. Optimised titles, meta descriptions, structured data for products, keyword-targeted category page copy, and fast load times that help Core Web Vitals. This is how e-commerce sites earn organic traffic without paying for every visit.
I have a small catalogue — is Sitezy still suitable?
Absolutely. Some of our best-performing e-commerce builds are for niche brands with under 20 products. A focused catalogue actually makes for a better, more conversion-optimised site than a sprawling one.
